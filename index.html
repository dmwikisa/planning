If the green line stays flat or rises, your savings keep their real value. If it drops below the dashed line, they're losing buying power.

The three careful settings: "Protect its value" reinvests enough each year to keep up with inflation and spends only the surplus — savings hold their buying power, income is lowest. "Keep the cash amount" spends all income but never the principal — the pula total stays flat, real value slips. "Just spend what I need" takes the full goal and may use up savings. Rates reflect mid-2026 Botswana (policy rate 5.5%, inflation ~8%, USD/BWP ~13.6). Not financial advice — review any plan with a fee-only advisor.
